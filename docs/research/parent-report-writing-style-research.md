## 1. Korean Daycare 알림장 (Daily Notes)

### What It Is
The 알림장 (notification journal) is a daily communication notebook between daycare/kindergarten teachers and parents. In Korea, most daycare centers now use the 키즈노트 (KidsNote) app for digital daily reports. Teachers write about each child's day -- meals, nap, activities, emotional state, social interactions, and developmental observations.

### Actual Example Texts

**Activity narrative with emotional detail:**
> "땡땡이는 블록을 쌓으면서 '높이 올라갔어!'라고 외치며 무척 기뻐했어요. 친구가 도와주자 '고마워'라고 말하며 협동의 기쁨도 함께 나누었답니다."
> (Ttangttangi was stacking blocks and shouted "It went up high!" with such joy. When a friend helped, they said "Thank you," sharing the joy of cooperation together.)

**Art activity with developmental note:**
> "미술 시간에 색종이를 오려서 예쁜 나비를 만들었어요. 가위 사용이 아직 익숙하지 않아 선생님과 함께 도와가며 활동했답니다."
> (During art time, they cut colored paper to make a pretty butterfly. Scissors aren't quite comfortable yet, so the teacher helped along the way.)

**Individual personality observation:**
> "땡땡이는 색종이를 고를 때 노란색을 가장 좋아했어요. 완성 후 친구에게 '내 나비 귀엽지?'라고 자랑했답니다."
> (Ttangttangi liked yellow the most when choosing colored paper. After finishing, they showed off to a friend saying "Isn't my butterfly cute?")

**Meal documentation:**
> "오늘 점심은 김치볶음밥이었는데 땡땡이는 두 그릇이나 뚝딱 비웠어요. 반찬 중에는 오이무침은 조금 남겼어요."
> (Today's lunch was kimchi fried rice and Ttangttangi polished off two whole bowls! Among the side dishes, they left a little of the cucumber salad.)

**Nap/condition report:**
> "11시쯤 잠이 들어 1시간 20분 정도 푹 잤어요. 일어난 후에도 컨디션이 좋아 친구들과 즐겁게 놀았답니다."
> (They fell asleep around 11 and slept deeply for about 1 hour and 20 minutes. After waking up, their condition was good and they played happily with friends.)

**Behavioral observation (specific, detailed):**
> "○○이는 식사 때나 잠을 잘 때, 장난감이 필요할 때면 '아아'라고 소리를 내서 자신의 의사를 표현합니다. 그래서 요즘에는 '주세요', '네' 등 짧은 단어를 반복해서 이야기해주면 익숙해지도록 도와주고 있어요."
> (At mealtimes, bedtime, or when they need a toy, OO makes the sound "ah-ah" to express themselves. So lately we've been repeating short words like "please" and "yes" to help them become familiar.)

**Non-punitive behavior redirection:**
> "장난감을 친구에게 던지는 행동에 대해 선생님과 조용히 이야기 나누었어요."
> (We had a quiet conversation about the behavior of throwing toys at friends.)

> "활동 중 작은 마찰이 있었지만, 서로 사과하고 잘 마무리했어요."
> (There was a small friction during activities, but they apologized to each other and wrapped it up well.)

**Weather-linked opening:**
> "오늘은 봄바람이 기분 좋게 불어오는 하루였습니다. 아이들도 바깥놀이에 웃음이 많았답니다."
> (Today was a day with a pleasant spring breeze. The children had lots of laughter during outdoor play.)

### Writing Techniques That Make It Warm

1. **Direct speech quotes** -- Recording the child's actual words ("높이 올라갔어!", "고마워") makes parents feel present
2. **Emotional descriptors** -- 기뻐했어요 (was joyful), 자랑했답니다 (proudly showed off), 즐겁게 (happily)
3. **답니다/답니다 sentence endings** -- The Korean suffix ~답니다 adds a storytelling quality, as if sharing a fond story
4. **Specific, concrete details** -- Not "ate well" but "polished off two bowls of kimchi fried rice"
5. **Name usage** -- Using the child's name (or nickname) throughout, not generic "your child"
6. **Developmental framing** -- Challenges described as growth opportunities, not deficits
7. **Teacher as partner** -- "선생님과 함께" (together with the teacher) positions teacher alongside child
8. **Gentle humor** -- Light, fond amusement at the child's personality quirks
9. **Engagement question** -- Ending with a question to parents: "가정에서는 ㅇㅇ이가 의사 표현 하나요?" (Does OO express themselves at home?)
10. **Varied descriptive verbs** -- 신나게 (excitedly), 집중해서 (concentratedly), 기뻐하며 (joyfully), 조심스럽게 (carefully)

### Information Conveyed Beyond Facts
- **Emotional temperature** of the child's day
- **Social dynamics** -- who they played with, how they interacted
- **Personality** -- preferences, habits, characteristic reactions
- **Growth trajectory** -- what they're getting better at
- **Caregiver's genuine affection** for the child

### Emotional Impact on Parents
- **Presence by proxy** -- feels like being there watching
- **Relief and reassurance** -- child is known, seen, cared for individually
- **Connection** -- parent can continue the conversation at home ("I heard you made a butterfly today!")
- **Trust** -- detailed observation = attentive care

## 2. Korean 생활기록부 (School Record Narratives)

### What It Is
The 생활기록부 is Korea's official school record. The 행동특성 및 종합의견 (Behavioral Characteristics and Comprehensive Opinion) section is a narrative evaluation written by the homeroom teacher, describing the student's character, growth, and overall development.

### Actual Example Texts

**Character-focused:**
> "온유한 성품으로 생각에 깊이가 있고 신중하게 행동하며 다른 사람의 감정을 배려할 줄 아는 따뜻한 마음씨를 지님"
> (With a gentle disposition, thinks deeply, acts carefully, and possesses a warm heart that knows how to be considerate of others' feelings)

**Social dynamics:**
> "일상에서 일어나는 사소한 일을 친구와 나누는 따뜻한 마음이 돋보임"
> (Shows a warm heart that stands out in sharing even small daily happenings with friends)

**Interpersonal:**
> "친구들과의 관계가 원만하고 분위기를 즐겁게 만드는 학생"
> (A student who has smooth relationships with friends and makes the atmosphere enjoyable)

**Responsibility:**
> "1년간 학급 학생들을 대표하여 가정통신문 등을 전달하는 역할을 성실하게 수행"
> (Faithfully carried out the role of representing the class and delivering parent communications for a full year)

### Key Writing Principles

1. **드라마틱 구조 (Dramatic structure):** "도전 --> 위기 --> 성공/실패 --> 깨달음" (Challenge --> Crisis --> Success/Failure --> Realization) -- showing growth as a narrative arc
2. **변화 가능성 (Potential for change):** When noting weaknesses, always frame them with the potential for growth
3. **구체적 근거 (Specific evidence):** Not just "good leader" but what specific behaviors demonstrate leadership
4. **명사형 어미 (Noun-form endings):** Korean narrative evaluations end in noun forms (~함, ~임, ~있음), giving a formal but observational quality
5. **Holistic view:** Character, values, personality, growth trajectory -- not just academic performance

### Relevance to Senior Care Reports
The 생활기록부 style shows how Korean educational writing:
- Balances positives with growth areas diplomatically
- Uses narrative structure (not checklists) to convey personality
- Frames observations with warmth and forward-looking optimism
- Conveys deep knowledge of the individual person

## 3. Japanese 連絡帳 (Renrakucho)

### What It Is
The 連絡帳 (renrakucho / communication notebook) is Japan's iconic daily communication system between daycare centers (保育園) and parents. A physical notebook (increasingly digital) where parents write about the child's home life on one side, and teachers write about the child's day on the other. It is arguably the world's gold standard for warm, detailed caregiver-to-parent communication.

### Actual Example Texts (Teacher Entries)

**Rich sensory narrative (0-year-old class):**
> "今日は、午前中お散歩に出かけました。○○くんはちょうちょやお花を指さして『あ！あ！』と何度も教えてくれました！"
> (Today we went for a walk in the morning. OO-kun pointed at butterflies and flowers, telling us "Ah! Ah!" again and again!)

**Emotional observation with warmth (infant):**
> "お友達が寝転んでころころしていると、■ちゃんもハイハイしていってころころ。上から保育士がレースをふわっとかけると、二人でキャッキャと笑ってとても可愛い■ちゃんでした。"
> (When a friend was lying down rolling around, XX-chan crawled over and rolled too. When the caregiver gently floated a lace cloth over them, the two laughed together with glee -- such an adorable XX-chan.)

**Empathy and kindness moment:**
> "不安で泣いているお友達に気づいて、積み木を放ってそっと寄ってきてくれた●くん。そのまま優しくよしよしと頭をなでてくれました。"
> (When ●-kun noticed a friend crying anxiously, he put down his blocks and gently came over. He softly patted their head to comfort them.)

**Persistence and growth:**
> "▲くんはブランコの柵につかまって頑張って立ち上がろうとしていました。何度も何度も挑戦していました。"
> (▲-kun was gripping the swing fence, trying hard to stand up. He challenged himself again and again.)

**Complex social dynamics (preschool):**
> "●くんは保育士や他のお友達に捕まえられると悔しさをにじましていましたが、仲良しの▲ちゃんに捕まえられると『あーあ捕まえられちゃったよ』と言いながらも少し嬉しそうな表情でした。"
> (When caught by the teacher or other friends, ●-kun showed frustration, but when caught by his close friend ▲-chan, despite saying "Aw, I got caught," he had a slightly happy expression.)

**Detailed discovery narrative:**
> "今日は、公園まで歩いてお散歩に行きました。芝生の中できれいな花を見つけて興味津々の〇〇ちゃん。においをかいで『おいしそうなにおいがする！』とワクワクした様子でした。園に帰って一番にお花の図鑑を見て、『〇〇が見つけたのはこのお花かな』と一生懸命探していましたよ"
> (Today we walked to the park. OO-chan found a beautiful flower in the grass and was very curious. She smelled it and said excitedly "It smells delicious!" When we got back, the first thing she did was look at the flower encyclopedia, earnestly searching "Is this the flower OO found?")

**Humor and surprise:**
> "おままごとで遊んでいるとき、お友だちに『このやさいは、ミネラルがほうふなんだよ！』と言っていた△△くん。お母さんたちのお話しを聞いていたのでしょうか？まさかの知識に、保育士も思わずビックリでした！"
> (While playing house, △△-kun told his friend "This vegetable is rich in minerals, you know!" Had he been listening to the moms' conversations? The caregiver was surprised by this unexpected knowledge!)

**Responding to parent concerns (child not socializing):**
> "今は▲くんは一人遊びに夢中ですが、時折、お友達と同じことをやって可愛い笑顔を見せてくれることもあります。少しずつお友達と同じ楽しみを分け合う姿が増えてきました。"
> (Right now ▲-kun is absorbed in solo play, but sometimes he does the same thing as friends and shows us a cute smile. Little by little, we're seeing more moments of him sharing the same fun with friends.)

**Reframing tantrums positively:**
> "イヤイヤは保育園でも見られていて、自分に少しずつ自信がついて何でもやりたくなった健康の印と思って、園でも見守っています。"
> (We see the "no-no" phase at daycare too. We think of it as a healthy sign that he's gradually gaining confidence and wants to do everything himself, and we're watching over him warmly.)

**Gentle concern with partnership:**
> "園では、お着替えの服を自分で選んでもらったり、『まだ遊びたいよね』などと声かけを行いながら、ゆっくりと関わりを持つようにしています。難しい場面もあるかもしれませんが、迷ったときは△△ちゃんの気持ちを代弁したり、選択肢を与えるなどして気持ちが落ち着くのを待ってみるのもいいかもしれません。何か不安なことがあればいつでもご相談くださいね"
> (At daycare, we let them choose their own clothes and slowly engage with them, saying things like "You still want to play, right?" There may be difficult moments, but when you're unsure, speaking for △△-chan's feelings or offering choices and waiting for calm might work well. If you're ever worried about anything, please always feel free to consult with us.)

### Writing Techniques Unique to Renrakucho

1. **Scene-painting** -- Teachers recreate specific moments so vividly parents can "see" the scene
2. **Direct speech in quotes** -- Child's actual words are quoted, creating intimacy
3. **Emotional nuance** -- Not just "happy" but the specific quality of happiness (嬉しそう, ワクワク, キャッキャ)
4. **Caregiver's own emotional response** -- "思わずビックリでした！" (I couldn't help but be surprised!) shares the caregiver's delight
5. **Growth reframing** -- Tantrums = "healthy sign of growing confidence"
6. **Partnership language** -- "一緒に見守っていきましょう" (Let's watch over them together)
7. **Open invitation** -- "いつでもご相談くださいね" (Please consult anytime)
8. **5W1H storytelling** -- When, where, who, what, why, how -- painting the full picture
9. **Personality portraiture** -- Building a consistent "character" of the child across days

### Emotional Impact
- Parents feel they **witnessed** the moment
- The notebook becomes a **treasured keepsake** -- parents often save them for years
- Creates deep **trust** between caregiver and family
- Makes parents feel **partnered** in caring, not excluded

## 4. International School Narrative Reports

### Montessori Progress Reports

**Core Philosophy (from Trillium Montessori):**
> "A thoughtfully written narrative is the best way to adequately convey the depth of knowledge you have about the child."

**Key Writing Principles:**

1. **The "Warm and Fuzzies" Matter:**
> "Parents want to know that you love their child and are moved by their special ways. Note specific incidents highlighting the child's unique reactions, including jokes they've made, kind words to a peer, or moments of wonder."

2. **5-to-1 Positive Ratio:**
> "Aim to provide 5-8 positive comments about the child for every single potentially negative comment you make."

3. **Observable Behavior, Not Diagnosis:**
> Write "Johnny often needs assistance to help him stay on task" rather than labeling him as "hyperactive." Specific behavioral descriptions convey issues more effectively than diagnostic language.

4. **Document Intervention Strategies:**
> When mentioning challenges, follow up with "a description of the strategies you have been using to address the issue, and the degree of success you have had with them."

5. **Holistic Child:**
> Include "social development, motor development, concentration, independence, work habits" -- not just academics.

6. **Track Progress Over Time:**
> Reference previous reports and celebrate improvements.

7. **No Surprises:**
> "There should be no unpleasant surprises in a written Montessori progress report." Discuss problems face-to-face first.

8. **Bookend with Positivity:**
> Start and end on encouraging notes.

### Preschool/Elementary Report Card Comments (Warm Examples)

**Social-emotional:**
> "_________ is confident, positive and a great role model for his/her classmates."
> "_________ relates well to classmates and is appreciative of different perspectives."
> "_________ manages his/her emotions maturely and responds to feedback appropriately."

**Character and growth:**
> "It has been a pleasure to have _________'s enthusiasm, positivity and maturity in my class."
> "_________ has shown excellent ability to set goals and be persistent in achieving them."

**Daily habits:**
> "_________ always looks for ways to be helpful in the classroom."
> "Your child is able to take responsibility for her own actions both in and out of the classroom."

### Relevance to Senior Care
The Montessori approach of **narrative over checklist**, **observable behavior over labels**, and **growth trajectory with specific anecdotes** translates directly to senior care reporting.

## 5. Care Home Family Updates

### Current State of Elderly Care Communication

**Caregiver daily logs typically track:**
- Medication & health (vitals, doses, side effects)
- Mood and mental wellbeing
- Activities and daily living (ADLs)
- Meals and nutrition
- Sleep patterns
- Behavioral changes

**The problem:** Most care home communication is clinical and checklist-based. It conveys facts but not personhood. There is a massive gap between how we communicate about children (warm, narrative, personality-rich) and how we communicate about elderly people (clinical, transactional, deficit-focused).

### What Works in Care Home Communication

**From Senior Living SMART (family update letter template):**
- Should be personalized to each resident
- Less than one page
- Include input from nurse, activities director, dining manager
- Use personable language
- Opening: "I am pleased to share this personal update with you about how [Name] is doing."

**From Go Icon (family engagement platform):**
- Automated push notifications when residents attend activities
- Photo sharing of daily moments
- Family members report feeling more connected

**From CareMobi (NYU-developed app):**
- Connects family caregivers, in-home caretakers, adult day care staff, and medical providers
- Goal: "getting everyone on the same team"

### Gap Identified
No existing service writes about elderly parents the way daycare teachers write about children. This is the core insight for Ondam -- **bridging the warmth gap in elder care communication.**

## 6. Korean Elder Care Service Reports

### Current Korean 주간보호센터 (Day Care Center for Elderly) Logs

**Actual example from a daily log:**

> **날짜:** 2024년 5월 15일
> **어르신 성명:** 김OO
>
> **신체:** "체온 36.8도, 혈압 140/90 mmHg, 맥박 72회/분. 오늘 아침부터 기침을 약간 하심."
> **정신:** "비교적 안정적인 상태 유지. 오전에는 활발하게 활동에 참여하셨으나, 오후에는 약간 피곤해하시는 모습."
> **행동:** "점심 식사 후 잠시 졸음. 오후 프로그램 시간에는 집중력이 약간 떨어지는 모습"
> **식사:** "아침, 점심 모두 양호하게 식사하심."
> **활동:** "오전 -- 신체 활동 (체조, 공놀이) 적극적으로 참여. 오후 -- 인지 활동 (색칠하기, 퍼즐) 참여했으나, 집중력이 떨어지는 모습"

**Analysis of this style:**
- Objective and clinical
- Uses respectful Korean honorifics (~하심, ~하시는 모습)
- Factual but lacks warmth, personality, or emotional color
- Reads like a medical chart, not a letter from someone who cares
- No anecdotes, no direct speech, no personality

### SKT NUGU Care Call (AI Elder Check-in)

AI-based phone call to elderly living alone:
- Asks: "어제 식사는 잘 하셨나요?" (Did you eat well yesterday?)
- Asks: "오늘 몸 상태는 괜찮으세요?" (Is your body feeling okay today?)
- Results shared with local government for follow-up
- Purely functional; no narrative or emotional warmth

### The Opportunity
Korean elder care communication is stuck in the clinical/checklist paradigm. There is no service that tells adult children: "Your mother laughed out loud today when she beat everyone at a card game, and then she shared her snack with the person sitting next to her, saying 'I'm in a good mood today.'" This narrative approach is exactly what daycare teachers do for children -- and what adult children desperately want for their parents.

## 7. What Adult Children (25-35) Want to Know

### Survey Data

**Scale of worry (Ring/UK survey):**
- 8 in 10 adults with aging parents worry about their welfare and security
- Over 86% worry about their parents up to 10 times per week
- When parent doesn't live with them, they carry guilt, worry, and loss of control

**Millennial caregiver burden (JubileeTV survey):**
- 59% of U.S. adults care for aging loved ones from a distance
- Millennials (25-44) most likely to provide daily remote assistance
- 1 in 3 spend 10+ hours/week on caregiving duties
- 66% report caregiving stress affects mental health
- 30% have less time for friends, hobbies, self-care

**Technology frustration:**
- 1 in 4 caregivers express dissatisfaction with available tools
- "Too complex for their loved ones or inadequate for managing caregiving needs"
- Key quote: "I could have avoided so many unnecessary trips if I'd had a reliable way to check in on my aunt from a distance"

### What They Actually Want to Know

Based on synthesis of caregiving research:

**Daily wellbeing (highest priority):**
1. Did they eat? What? How much?
2. Did they sleep well?
3. What was their mood like today?
4. Did they interact with anyone? Who?
5. Did they go outside / get any activity?

**Health monitoring:**
6. Any changes in physical condition?
7. Are they taking medications?
8. Any falls, pain, or new symptoms?

**Emotional/social wellbeing:**
9. Do they seem lonely or withdrawn?
10. Are they engaged in activities they enjoy?
11. Did they laugh, smile, express pleasure?
12. Do they talk about family/past/future?

**Cognitive indicators (subtle):**
13. Can they follow conversations?
14. Do they remember things from yesterday?
15. Any confusion or disorientation?

**What they DON'T want:**
- Medical jargon without context
- Only hearing about problems
- Being contacted only for emergencies
- Checkbox-style reports with no personality

## 8. Psychology of Peace-of-Mind Reporting

### What Reduces Worry

1. **Regular rhythm** -- Daily updates at a consistent time create routine and predictability. A daily text or message gives peace of mind.
2. **Specificity** -- "Your mother ate two bowls of soup and said it was delicious" is more reassuring than "Ate well"
3. **Personality visible** -- When the report captures the parent's personality (humor, preferences, habits), children feel their parent is still "themselves"
4. **Social connection evidence** -- Knowing their parent is not isolated, that they interacted with others
5. **Activity and engagement** -- Signs of mental/physical activity signal vitality
6. **Caregiver warmth** -- Evidence that the caregiver genuinely likes/respects the parent
7. **Continuity** -- References to previous days/weeks show ongoing awareness, not just a snapshot

### What Increases Worry

1. **Silence** -- No communication is the worst. The imagination fills the gap with fear
2. **Only negative updates** -- If every message is about problems, children dread notifications
3. **Vague language** -- "Not feeling well" without specifics creates anxiety spirals
4. **Clinical tone** -- Medical language without emotional context makes the parent feel like a patient, not a person
5. **Comparison to "normal"** -- "Ate less than usual" without context ("but enjoyed the fruit and asked for more") is alarming
6. **Information without action** -- "Seemed sad today" with no follow-up about what was done

### The "Emotional Temperature" Principle

The most effective peace-of-mind communication answers one core question: **"Is my parent okay, not just physically but as a person?"**

This means conveying:
- Physical status (eating, sleeping, moving)
- Emotional state (mood, engagement, social connection)
- Personhood (preferences, humor, choices, agency)

## 9. Sensitive Information Delivery

### How Daycare Teachers Handle Difficult Topics

Korean 알림장 approach:
- **Non-punitive phrasing:** "선생님과 조용히 이야기 나누었어요" (We had a quiet conversation) instead of "was disciplined"
- **Context sandwich:** Positive observation --> concern --> positive outlook
- **Partnership framing:** "집에서도 다시 한번 이야기 부탁드립니다" (Please talk about it again at home too) -- shared responsibility

Japanese renrakucho approach:
- **Reframing:** Tantrums = "healthy sign of growing confidence"
- **Intervention transparency:** Describing what the teacher did and how it worked
- **Open door:** "何か不安なことがあればいつでもご相談くださいね" (If anything worries you, please consult anytime)

### Montessori Approach to Concerns

- **5:1 ratio** -- Five positives for every concern
- **Observable behavior** -- "needs assistance staying on task" not "is hyperactive"
- **Strategy documentation** -- Show what you're doing about it
- **No surprises** -- Major concerns discussed in person first, not dropped into written reports
- **Bookend positively** -- Start and end with warmth

### Application to Senior Care

**Instead of:** "어머님이 오늘 식사를 잘 안 하셨습니다."
(Your mother didn't eat well today.)

**Write:** "오전에 체조 시간에 팔을 크게 흔들며 활기차게 참여하셨어요. 점심은 국을 두 숟갈 정도 드시고 '입맛이 없네' 하셨는데, 오후 간식으로 나온 고구마는 '이건 맛있다' 하시며 다 드셨답니다. 내일은 어머님이 좋아하시는 된장찌개가 나오니 기대해 주세요."
(She participated energetically in morning exercises, swinging her arms wide. At lunch she had about two spoonfuls of soup and said "I don't have much appetite," but when sweet potato came out as an afternoon snack, she said "This is delicious" and ate it all. Tomorrow her favorite doenjang-jjigae is on the menu, so let's look forward to it.)

**Techniques used:**
- Positive bookend (energetic exercise)
- Specific detail (two spoonfuls, her actual words)
- Honest about the concern (didn't eat much)
- Recovery within the same report (ate the snack happily)
- Forward-looking positive (favorite food tomorrow)
- Her personality shines (her actual words quoted)

## 10. Synthesis: Writing Style Guide for Ondam

### Core Concept
**"어머님/아버님의 오늘 하루" (Your Parent's Day Today)** -- A daily KakaoTalk message that reads like a daycare 알림장, but for elderly parents. Warm, narrative, personality-rich, with the care and detail of a Japanese 連絡帳.

### Tone
- **따뜻한 이웃 (Warm neighbor)** -- Like a kind younger person who genuinely enjoys spending time with their parent
- Not clinical, not formal, not condescending
- Respectful Korean honorifics for the parent (어머님, ~하셨어요, ~드셨답니다)
- Casual-warm tone toward the adult child reader (like a friend updating you)

### Structure (KakaoTalk Message Format)

```
[온담] 어머님의 오늘 하루 (3월 24일 월요일)

🌤 오늘의 한마디
"어머님이 오늘 산책하시면서 '벚꽃이 벌써 폈네, 우리 딸한테 사진 보내줘' 하셨어요."

📋 오늘 하루
오전에 체조 시간에 평소보다 활기차게 참여하셨어요.
특히 스트레칭할 때 옆에 계신 분과 "누가 더 잘 하나 내기해볼까?"
하시며 웃으셨답니다.

점심은 된장찌개와 고등어구이였는데,
"고등어는 역시 가을이 제일 맛있지" 하시면서도
깨끗하게 드셨어요.

오후에는 화투를 치셨는데,
이기셨을 때 "나 아직 머리 잘 돌아가지?" 하시며
즐거워하셨답니다.

💚 컨디션
식사 양호 | 활동 적극적 | 기분 좋음

한마디 더: 요즘 산책을 특히 좋아하세요.
주말에 같이 걸어보시면 좋아하실 것 같아요.
```

### Writing Rules

1. **반드시 직접 인용 포함** (Always include direct quotes)
   - The parent's actual words are the soul of the report
   - "입맛이 없네" > "식욕이 떨어지셨습니다"

2. **구체적 장면 묘사** (Paint specific scenes)
   - Not "활동에 참여하셨습니다" (participated in activities)
   - But "화투를 치시면서 이기실 때마다 손뼉을 치셨어요" (While playing hwatu, she clapped every time she won)

3. **감정의 온도 전달** (Convey emotional temperature)
   - How did they seem? Happy, calm, energetic, tired, quiet?
   - Use specific Korean emotional descriptors: 활기차게, 즐거워하시며, 평온하게, 조용히

4. **성격이 보이는 글쓰기** (Writing that shows personality)
   - Their humor, preferences, habits, characteristic phrases
   - The goal: the reader should "hear" their parent's voice

5. **걱정은 맥락과 함께** (Concerns always with context)
   - Never isolated bad news
   - Always: what happened + what we did + what came after
   - Forward-looking positive whenever possible

6. **파트너십 언어** (Partnership language)
   - "같이 지켜봐 주세요" (Let's watch together)
   - "주말에 ~하시면 좋아하실 것 같아요" (They'd probably enjoy if you ~ this weekend)
   - Subtle engagement prompts, not instructions

7. **5:1 비율** (5:1 positive ratio)
   - Five warm/positive observations for every concern
   - Even on difficult days, find the light

8. **연속성** (Continuity)
   - Reference previous days: "어제 말씀하셨던 ~"
   - Track ongoing interests/projects
   - Show you remember and care about the whole person

### What NOT to Do

- Medical jargon without translation to human terms
- Checklist format (식사: O, 활동: O, 수면: O)
- Generic templates with no personalization
- Only contacting when something is wrong
- Treating the elderly parent as a passive object of care
- Condescending language that strips away dignity
- Sharing information that would embarrass the parent

### The Emotional Promise to Adult Children
Every message should implicitly say:
> "당신의 부모님은 오늘도 한 사람의 인격체로서 존중받으며, 웃고, 먹고, 활동하고, 사람들과 어울리며 하루를 보내셨습니다. 그리고 저희는 그 모든 순간을 곁에서 지켜보고 있습니다."
> (Your parent spent today being respected as a full person -- laughing, eating, being active, socializing. And we were beside them through all of it.)
